Places to Visit in Rajasthan

Nov 6th 2023 4 Min read

Leh Ladakh is an undeveloped but breathtaking tourist destination in the Great Himalayas. The rough valleys, mountains, meandering roads, and thriving cultural life of maintain is the region’s vibrancy and charm. The famous Magnetic Hill, the turquoise-colored Pangong Lake, the meeting of two mysterious rivers, the oldest and most revered monasteries in Ladakh, and the highest passes are just a few spectacular sights in the Leh Ladakh bike trip route as a whole.

1. Finest Time To Visit Ladakh

The best time for Ladakh bike trip is from May to September. That is when the roads are open, and the weather is relatively mild. During this time, the average temperature ranges from 15°C to 25°C during the day and drops to as low as -5°C at night in the higher altitudes.

Close To Heaven, Down To Earth.

2. Choosing The Right Bike

Choosing the right Bike for a Leh Ladakh bike trip is crucial. The Bike must be sturdy enough to handle the rough terrain and high altitude. Popular bikes for this trip are Royal Enfield, KTM, and Himalayan. It is essential to ensure that the Bike is in good condition and serviced before the trip. Carrying spare parts, such as tubes, cables, and spark plugs is also recommended.

3.Necessary Permits

Traveling to Leh Ladakh On a Bike Trip requires several permits. Citizens of India need an Inner Line Permit (ILP) to enter certain areas in Ladakh. Likewise, foreign nationals are required to produce a Protected Area Permit (PAP) to visit certain regions in Ladakh. These permits can be obtained from the Ladakh Autonomous Hill Development Council (LAHDC) office in Leh or online through the official website.

Accommodation

Hotels in Leh Ladakh range from budget-friendly homestays in Leh to luxurious hotels. It is advisable to reserve accommodation in advance during peak season. Many travellers prefer to carry their camping gear and camp at scenic locations.
